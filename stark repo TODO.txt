To clarify for users
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
	- It's all an approximation (numerical) of the approximation (modeling).
		Some models and numerical solutions are easy to get to work quite well, others not so much.
		Friction is particularly problematic in both departments:
			a) The model is a very rough approximation or reality. Will work well for tidy contacts, but will be problematic for huge velocities and stickiness (despite what papers say).
				Since it is "lagged", it can be very time step dependent.
			b) It is numerically challenging, specially when the stick-slide condition is tightly define.
		In highly dynamis scenes (car) small time steps and tight tolerances can make a big difference.
	- Still sometimes the codegen folder needs to be recomputed or the program run again.
	- The performance numbers from the paper might not be 100% accurate anymore as improvements to safety and other more flexible facilities were added.
	- Can't remove objects
