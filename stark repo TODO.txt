Convergence improvements
------------------------
Notes:
	The bottleneck is clearly that I have am given many non-descend directions. That determines the time step size 100%.
	Trying negative direction was slower and caused other problems.
	I can check if the directions are also too long. Like, bad in general, not just orientation.

Ideas:
	- Higher order mollifiers ?
	- Better and less stringent Newton and CG tolerance
	- Flip search directions when uphill
		- This has a direct influence on how the increase in barrier stiffness works as now you get arbitrary directions to handle
		This really didn't work better at all.
		I think I am getting bad directions in general. Not just wrong orientation but also too large.
		Then the is_valid_step() struggles and we end up discarding the step.
		It might be that it it works for Andreas but it is also slower for him. We just don't know.
		It is definitely slower for me. It is faster to accept that I don't get a good direction with the current circumstances and I should reduce the time step to get comfy.
		
	- Better descend methods (NewtonCG?)
	- Newton's method needs a lot of work in understanding, convergence, when to repeat a time step...
		* NewtonsMethod is a mess
			callbacks and evaluation should go together
			does it make sense that it is it's own class? Maybe a function is better?

	* The criteria to increase the stiffness is not sound. It is seriously overestimating it and has many
		unnecessary time step repeats.
		Actually I am not sure about this. See next point.
	* Rigid bodies are much harder on the collisions because they cannot store potential energy.
		Soft bodies will deform so the collision energy only takes part of the impact.
		Maybe a solution is affine bodies or to model damping forces when rigid bodies collide.
		Also smaller time steps might make things worse as they divide the inertia force:
			to completely stop a rigid object in one ms, one needs a huge force.
				f = -1/dt*(v1 - vhat)*m
		
	* For rigid bodies, collision detection runtime becomes massively relevant
		Slowest part is the line search as I need to run CD every evaluation.
		Consider that for the paper about it.
