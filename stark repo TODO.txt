Rigid body stiffness situation
------------------------------
I need to understand rigid body dynamics much better, specially their relation with force.

With cloth I could use very low barrier stiffnesses because it is very light and the impact gets partially absorved by the elasticity/damping.
For a 1kg box falling 20cm to a fix plane, I need a very high force to stop it on its tracks within one time step.
Look at the equation for the inertia:
	E = 0.5*m*(v1 - vhat).dot(v1 - vhat)  ->  f = -dE/dx = -(v1 - vhat)*m/dt
Stopping a 1kg box coming at 1m/s in 1ms requires 1000N of force (100kg).
Using a 1mm barrier thickness, one would need 3.3e8.

Another problem is reducing the time step.
Everytime this happens, the inertia forces become stronger.
I don't reduce the time step anymore when invalid configuration, but still is annoying.
If a barrier changes very significantly, it will overshoot. I cannot just start with 1e3 and let it build to 1e10.
